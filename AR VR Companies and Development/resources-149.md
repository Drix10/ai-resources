### 🤖 Game Development - UEFN/UE Optimization Techniques

This article explores optimization techniques for User-Generated Content (UGC) projects within UEFN and Unreal Engine. It focuses on reducing actor counts and mitigating FPS drops to improve performance on lower-end PCs and mobile devices.

Key Points:

• Optimization is crucial for UEFN/UE projects to ensure broad device compatibility.

• Reducing actor count directly improves game performance.

• Addressing FPS drops enhances the user experience on less powerful hardware.

• Applying optimization techniques benefits both PC and mobile users.


🚀 Implementation:
1. Profile Performance: Use built-in tools to identify performance bottlenecks.
2. Optimize Actor Count: Consolidate meshes, use instancing, and cull unnecessary actors.
3. Reduce Draw Calls: Batch materials and minimize unique texture usage.
4. Implement LODs: Create Level of Detail (LOD) assets for distant objects.
5. Cull Occluded Geometry: Utilize occlusion culling to prevent rendering unseen elements.
