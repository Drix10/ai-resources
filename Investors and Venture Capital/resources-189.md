### 🤖 Game Development - Floating Point Precision

This article explains a common issue in 3D rendering related to floating-point precision and distant geometry. It details why objects far from the world origin can exhibit visual artifacts.

Key Points:

• float32 precision decreases significantly at large distances from the world origin.

• Distant geometry can visibly swim or flicker due to this reduced precision.

• This artifact is caused by floating-point jitter, not faulty models.

• Keeping active rendered geometry near the world origin mitigates these issues.


🚀 Implementation:
1. Design game levels or environments to cycle active geometry near the origin.
2. Implement systems to relocate or stream sections of the world dynamically.
3. Ensure that relevant dungeon slots or active areas are always positioned close to the fixed world origin.
