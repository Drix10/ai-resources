### 💡 Public Health - New World Screwworm Resurgence

This article details the re-emergence of the New World screwworm in the United States after approximately 60 years. It provides essential information regarding the current situation, risks to humans and livestock, and necessary precautions.

Key Points:

• New World screwworm has reappeared in the US after six decades.

• Risk to human health is low, but open wounds require careful management.

• The food supply remains safe as screwworms do not infest meat or produce.

• Livestock owners must inspect animals for infestations and report suspicions promptly.

• Quarantine of infected animals is crucial to control further spread.

🚀 Implementation:
1. Inspect Livestock: Regularly check cattle, sheep, goats, and pets for draining wounds and maggots.
2. Observe Wounds: Pay close attention to newborn navels, castration or branding sites, and any open cuts.
3. Seek Veterinary Care: Treat any suspected cases under the guidance of a veterinarian.
4. Report Suspicions: Report any confirmed or suspected screwworm cases to TAHC or USDA within 24 hours.
5. Practice Personal Hygiene: Keep open wounds clean and covered, especially after visiting outbreak areas.
